## Understanding the Historical Impact of Rates on the economy

Dating back to the Babylonians, interest rates have long served as a crucial lever in the economy.
Interest rates influence everything from consumer spending to business investment to international capital flows. 
Historically, central banks have used interest rates to curb inflation, stimulate economic growth, or stabilize financial markets. 
But significant shifts in interest rates often coincide with major economic events, highlighting their pivotal role in shaping economic landscapes. 
In the following section, we'll explore several historical case studies that demonstrate how changes in interest rates have affected the economy in the past, 
offering valuable insights into the dynamics of financial policy and economic response.
